PURCHASING MANAGER
Duties, Key Skills and Competencies

Procurement Management

  • High standard of IT literacy.

  • An excellent communicator both internally and externally.

  • Ability to influence and manage across multiple international cultures.

  • A strategic thinker who confidently solves problems with informed, timely and decisive decisions across a broad range of supply chains.

  • Managing a high volume of turnover.

  • Excellent negotiating and influencing skills.

Duties:

  • Tracking and reporting key locations to reduce expenses and improve effectiveness and negotiate commercial agreements.

  • Attending meetings, trade shows and conferences to learn about new industry trends, purchase products and make contacts with new suppliers.

  • Ensuring management systems are maintained accurately.

  • Maintaining and developing stock holding.

  • Ensuring that all purchasing targets agreed with suppliers are met and that all rebate earning potential is achieved.

  • Preparing an annual plan in liaison with the management team, to reduce expenditure on a sustainable basis.

  • Stock and materials management, inventory turns, stock takes and stock rotation.

  • Analysing stock holding and forecasting demand to leverage best deals with suppliers.

  • Meeting the needs of the department and the company.

  • Positively influencing juniors and peers alike to ensure compliance with procurement strategy, policy and process.

  • Forecasting price and market trends to identify changes of balance in buyer-supplier power.

  • Developing close relationship with suppliers, whilst also employing robust negotiating skills.

  • Negotiating terms and developing purchase strategies for each supplier.
